# Amala and Ewedu: The Nigerian Yoruba Delicacy

If you're a fan of Nigerian cuisine, chances are you've come across the delicious combination of amala and ewedu. This traditional Yoruba dish is a staple in many Nigerian homes and is enjoyed by people of all ages. I came about this delicacy at an early age and whilst I'm not a super fan. I do enjoy it a LOT! 🤩

Amala is a type of yam flour that is made by grinding yams into a fine powder. It is then mixed with water to form a dough-like consistency, which is formed into small balls and cooked in boiling water. Amala has a slightly earthy taste and a soft, slightly chewy texture. It is often served as a side dish or as a base for other dishes.

![](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1673001853518/e4f61910-0ea9-4052-b194-360122f249be.jpeg align="center")

Ewedu, on the other hand, is a type of leafy green vegetable that is native to West Africa. It is similar in appearance to okra and has a slimy texture when cooked. Ewedu is usually prepared by boiling the leaves and then pounding them into a smooth, green paste. It is often served alongside amala and is known for its mild, slightly bitter taste 💀.

Together, amala and ewedu make for a hearty and satisfying meal 🤤. The combination of the slightly earthy and chewy amala with the mild and slightly bitter ewedu is a match made in heaven. The dish is often served with a spicy stew or sauce, such as egusi or ogiri okpei, which adds an extra layer of flavor to the dish.

Amala is a good source of complex carbohydrates, while ewedu is rich in vitamins and minerals such as vitamin A, vitamin C, and iron. This makes the dish a great option for those looking to incorporate more healthy and nourishing meals into their diet.

Overall, **amala and ewedu** is a must-try for anyone looking to experience the rich and diverse flavors of Nigerian cuisine. Whether you're a fan of traditional dishes or just looking to try something new, this delicious and nutritious combination is sure to satisfy your taste buds👌.
