# Developers, Light vs Dark Mode 💀

The question of why developers hate the light and prefer dark mode has been asked for years. Developers have always had a strong affinity for the dark, from its mysterious and alluring nature to the fact that the light just seems so…bright. But why do developers feel this way? Well, let me tell you a tale.

Once upon a time, in the land of source code, there lived a group of developers. They were happy and productive people, coding away to their hearts' content. But one day, disaster struck.

The sun rose in the sky, casting its harsh rays upon the land. The developers, accustomed to the soothing darkness of their code editors, were blinded by the light. They stumbled and fumbled, their fingers trembling as they tried to type.

"This is intolerable!" cried one developer. "We must do something about this light!"

And so, the developers set to work. They toiled and labored, pouring over their code and their design tools. And finally, after many long days and nights, they emerged triumphant.

"Behold!" they cried. "We have created the perfect solution – dark mode!"

With a flick of a switch, the developers transformed their code editors and design tools into dark and mysterious realms. The harsh light of the sun was banished, and the developers could once again see clearly.

What is it about the dark that appeals to them? The answer lies in the fact that developers are very visual people. They spend hours upon hours staring at a computer screen analyzing code, designing user interfaces, and debugging programs. The bright light of a computer monitor or the overhead lights of a workspace can quickly become overwhelming and cause eye strain, headaches, and fatigue. The dark offers a retreat from the harsh light of the day, providing a more comfortable environment for developers to work in.

The dark also helps to reduce the amount of glare on the monitor, which can make it easier to focus on the coding task at hand. Additionally, the dark can be used to create a more calming atmosphere, allowing developers to stay focused on their work longer.

In addition to the visual benefits, the dark mode also offers several advantages that appeal to developers. For example, it can help reduce the amount of energy used by the computer, saving on electricity bills.

Dark mode can also make the text easier to read, which is important for developers who often have to read through lines of code.

Finally, dark mode can help to reduce eye strain and fatigue, which can be especially beneficial for those who work long hours.

Overall, dark mode offers several benefits that make it an attractive option for developers. It provides a more comfortable and calming environment, reduces glare and eyestrain, and can even save energy. In the end, it’s easy to see why developers prefer dark mode over light. So, if you’re a developer and you’re looking for a way to make your work environment more comfortable, consider switching over to dark mode. You’ll be glad you did!
